Places and powers

Worlds, Factions, and Technology

Tatooine

Desert world in the Outer Rim, ruled more by survival, moisture farming, criminal power, and local tribes than central government. It shapes Anakin and Luke through isolation, hardship, and longing.

Coruscant

Galactic capital, city-planet, Senate seat, Jedi Temple home, and visual symbol of civilization layered over inequality. Its skyline hides political decay underneath ceremonial grandeur.

Naboo

Elegant world of humans and Gungans. Its invasion launches the prequel crisis, introduces Padme and Palpatine's political opportunity, and contrasts beauty with manipulation.

Mandalore

A warrior culture split by pacifism, clan identity, occupation, exile, and myth. The Darksaber becomes a contested symbol of leadership rather than a simple crown.

Rebel Alliance

A coalition of senators, spies, soldiers, defectors, smugglers, and citizens. Its strength is distributed courage; its challenge is balancing secrecy, sacrifice, and hope.

Galactic Empire

A centralized dictatorship built from the Republic's legal machinery. It uses governors, stormtroopers, intelligence, propaganda, fear, and industrial scale to hold systems in line.

First Order

Imperial remnants transformed into a militarized successor state. It relies on indoctrination, hidden buildup, fanatic command, and resentment toward the New Republic.

Lightsabers

Jedi and Sith ritual weapons powered by kyber crystals. Blade color often signals tradition, construction, corruption, or personal path: blue and green for Jedi practice, red for bled crystals, white for purified ones.
