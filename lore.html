Lore, Rules, and Conflicts

The Force, politics, war machines, and technology rules are grouped here so the explanation side of the archive has its own home.

The Force in detail

The Force connects living beings, places, memory, instinct, and destiny. Jedi training emphasizes calm perception, service, and letting go of possessive fear. Dark side practice gives quick power because it feeds on intense emotion, but it narrows the user's world until control replaces connection.

Common abilities include precognition, telekinesis, heightened reflexes, mind influence, sensing emotions, healing, and communion with the dead. Rare or dangerous practices include Sith alchemy, essence transfer, Force lightning, dyads, and artifact-bound knowledge.

Jedi Order

Peacekeepers trained from youth, organized by temples, councils, masters, knights, and padawans. Their strengths are discipline and service. Their weakness is institutional blindness when politics and war reshape their role.

Sith Order

Dark side tradition built around power, secrecy, domination, and succession by betrayal. Darth Bane's Rule of Two reduces Sith infighting into one master and one apprentice until Palpatine reveals the final trap.

Clone Wars

A manufactured conflict between the Republic and Separatists. Both armies are shaped by Sith planning: clones on one side, battle droids on the other, and emergency powers feeding Palpatine's rise.

Hyperspace

Ships travel faster than light through calculated routes. Navigation matters because gravity wells, mass shadows, interdiction fields, and bad coordinates can destroy a vessel or strand it in hostile space.

Droids

Droids range from protocol interpreters and astromechs to assassins and tactical units. They are tools in law, but many display memory, loyalty, humor, fear, and personality shaped by experience.

Superweapons

The Death Stars, Starkiller Base, and planet-killing fleets show authoritarian thinking at its most extreme: reduce politics to fear, then mistake terror for permanent control.
